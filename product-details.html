Product Description

Chanel No. 5 is the first perfume launched by French couturier Gabrielle "Coco" Chanel in 1921. The scent formula for the fragrance was compounded by French-Russian chemist and perfumer Ernest Beaux. The design of its bottle has been an important part of the product's branding.

Top Notes

Heart Notes

Iris, Jasmine, Rose, Orris Root, Lily of the Valley

Base Notes

Sandalwood, Vanilla, Amber, Patchouli, Vetiver

About the Scent

An abstract, mysterious scent, alive with a bouquet of subtle florals. The essence of a timeless symbol of elegance and femininity.

How to Use

1
Apply to Pulse Points

Spray perfume on your wrists, neck, behind ears, and inner elbows where blood vessels are closest to the skin.

2
Don't Rub

Allow the perfume to dry naturally on your skin. Rubbing can break down the molecular structure and alter the scent.

3
Moisturize First

Apply to moisturized skin to help the fragrance last longer. Perfume evaporates faster on dry skin.

4
Layer Your Scent

For maximum longevity, use matching body lotion or shower gel from the same fragrance line.

Pro Tips
  • Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight
  • Keep the bottle sealed when not in use to preserve the fragrance
  • Spray from 6-8 inches away for even distribution
  • Reapply after 6-8 hours if needed
